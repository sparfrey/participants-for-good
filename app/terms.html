Participant terms

The promises between you and Participants for Good ("PFG"), a nonprofit participant network.

1. Who can join

You must be 18 or older (or the age of majority where you live) and provide accurate profile information. One account per person.

2. Participation is always your choice

Every invitation is optional. Declining, skipping questions, or leaving a study early never affects your standing, and you may close your account at any time.

3. How you get paid

  • You receive the stated incentive for every session you complete, paid promptly after the researcher confirms completion, with no minimum balance and no expiry.
  • If a researcher cancels late or fails to attend, you are still paid for the scheduled session.
  • If you miss a session without notice, that session is unpaid; repeated no-shows may pause new invitations.
  • You are responsible for any taxes on incentive income; we provide required tax forms at applicable thresholds.

4. Contributions to your cause

For every session you complete, PFG makes a separate contribution to the verified nonprofit you selected. This contribution comes from the research fee, never from your pay, and is made by PFG, so it is not your personal donation and is not tax-deductible by you.

5. Your information

  • Profile answers (including optional demographic questions) are used only to match you with studies and to report aggregate panel statistics.
  • Researchers see your first name and the study criteria you matched, never your contact details, and never your full profile.
  • We never sell your data. You can view, correct, or delete your information at any time.

6. Consent, every study

Each study has its own plain-language consent describing what happens, whether it's recorded, and how your responses are used. You can withdraw consent during a session and still be paid for time spent.

7. We limit how often you're asked

To keep participation fair and pressure-free, we cap the number of studies any person can join per quarter. The cap is shown in your dashboard.

8. Your part

Give honest answers, keep study materials confidential when asked, and don't attempt to game screeners or hold multiple accounts. Violations may end the relationship.

9. Changes & termination

We'll notify you of material changes to these terms. Either of us can end the relationship at any time; you keep everything you've earned.

Nonprofit partner terms

The partnership between your organization and Participants for Good ("PFG").

1. Eligibility & verification

Partners must be registered charitable or nonprofit organizations. We verify status (e.g., EIN or local registry) before any funds are paid; contributions accrue during verification.

2. What the partnership is

Supporters and members of the public may select your organization as their cause. For every research session they complete, PFG contributes to your organization. There are no quotas, no minimums, and no fees to you.

3. Contributions & payouts

  • Contributions are unrestricted charitable distributions from PFG, paid monthly by bank transfer with an itemized statement.
  • Amounts depend entirely on completed sessions; nothing is guaranteed.
  • An annual summary suitable for your records and board reporting is provided.

4. Your name & brand

You control how your name is used. We use it only to let supporters select you and to report totals; anything beyond that (logos, co-marketing, appearing in a study's description) requires your approval, and you may withdraw approval anytime.

5. Protecting your community

  • You may block study categories from reaching your supporters (e.g., alcohol, gambling, political research).
  • Your supporters are always paid, always consent-first, and protected by participation caps.
  • We never give you or anyone else a supporter's individual research activity; you see aggregate counts and funds only.

6. What we ask of you

Share your invite link honestly: don't pay, pressure, or require anyone to sign up, and don't present participation as a condition of receiving your services.

7. Ending the partnership

Either party may end the partnership at any time. Accrued contributions are paid out on the normal schedule; supporters who selected you may keep or change their cause.

Research client terms

The agreement between your team and Participants for Good ("PFG") when you commission recruiting.

1. The service

PFG recruits, schedules, and compensates research participants for your studies. We are a nonprofit: fees are split between participant incentives, contributions to participant-chosen nonprofits, and operations, and the split is disclosed on every estimate and invoice.

2. Fees & payment

  • Fees are due before fielding begins (invoice/PO terms available for approved organizations).
  • Completed sessions are billed as quoted; unused backups are credited.
  • If you cancel a scheduled session with less than 24 hours' notice, the participant is still paid and that session is charged.

3. Participant protections you agree to honor

  • Sessions follow the approved consent scope: no recording, no sensitive topics, and no data collection beyond what was approved.
  • You will not ask participants for contact information, recruit them off-platform, or re-contact them outside PFG.
  • Research data you collect is used for your stated research purpose only and never sold or used to build audience or advertising profiles of participants.

4. What you can see

You receive a participant's first name and the study criteria they matched, nothing more. Contact details, full profiles, and scheduling logistics stay with PFG. Panel statistics you see are aggregate.

5. Study review

Studies are subject to a brief ethics review (consent language, incentive fairness, topic flags). We may decline or require changes to studies that put participants or partner communities at risk.

6. Impact reporting

We report the contributions generated by your studies in aggregate, including in our public open-books reporting. We won't name your organization publicly without permission.

7. Confidentiality

We keep your study materials and unreleased product information confidential; you keep participant information and session content within your research team.

8. Liability & changes

Recruiting timelines are estimates, not guarantees. Our liability is capped at fees paid for the affected study. We'll notify you of material changes to these terms.
